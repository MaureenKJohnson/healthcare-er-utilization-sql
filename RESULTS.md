## Results

### ER Utilization by Insurance Type

Across all insurance groups, ER utilization varied modestly. Self-Pay patients demonstrated the highest average number of ER visits per patient, while Medicaid patients demonstrated the lowest overall ER utilization. Medicare and Medicare Advantage patients showed intermediate utilization rates.

### Hospital Admission Rates

Hospital admission rates differed by insurance type. Medicaid patients exhibited the highest proportion of ER visits resulting in hospital admission, while Self-Pay patients had the lowest admission rates. Admission rates among Medicare, Medicare Advantage, and Commercial insurance groups were similar.

### 30-Day Repeat ER Visits

Repeat ER visits within a 30-day period were more common among Medicare and Medicare Advantage patients compared to other insurance groups. Commercially insured patients demonstrated the lowest repeat-visit rates. Medicaid patients had relatively low repeat-visit rates in this dataset.
